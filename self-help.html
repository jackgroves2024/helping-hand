Support Your Own Mental Health Journey

The Physical Approach

Regular Exercise

Regular exercise boosts mental health by reducing stress, improving mood, enhancing sleep, and increasing overall feelings of well-being through the release of endorphins and reduced anxiety levels.

Start small, even as little as 10-15min a day can help. Work your way up to 30 minutes, 3-5 days a week.

Quality Sleep

Quality sleep is essential for mental health, as it enhances emotional regulation, reduces stress, and improves mood, focus, and overall cognitive function.

Aim for 7-9 hours of sleep a night. Try to leave time to unwind before bed. Relax, maybe read a book. Keep away from screens!

Balanced Diet

A balanced diet plays a crucial role in mental health by providing the body with essential nutrients which help improve brain function, stabilize mood, and reduce the risk of anxiety, depression, and more.

Don't be afraid to be adventurous! A balanced diet doesn't mean starve yourself, or only eating greens.

Going Outside

Spending time outdoors in nature reduces stress, improves mood, and boosts mental clarity by promoting relaxation, and fostering a sense of connection with the environment.

Even if it starts with a light change of scenery, sitting in your garden can be enough to settle down and freshen up.

The Mindful Approach

Meditation

Mindfulness improves mental health by helping individuals stay present, reduce stress, enhance emotional regulation, and increase self-awareness, leading to greater emotional resilience and well-being.

You can find out more about engaging with this technique from the NHS here

Breathing Exercises

Breathing exercises help improve mental health by reducing stress, calming the nervous system, enhancing focus, and promoting emotional balance through deep relaxation and controlled breathing techniques.

To practice box breathing, inhale deeply for 4 seconds, hold your breath for 4 seconds, exhale slowly for 4 seconds, and hold again for 4 seconds, then repeat.

Journaling

Writing down thoughts and feelings in a journal can help process emotions, gain perspective, and release pent-up stress. It also promotes self-reflection and can help track progress in mental well-being.

Try to start by keeping daily journal entries, expressing how you feel with honesty and freedom. Don't be afraid to write negatives AND positives!

Cognitive Behavioral Therapy (CBT)

CBT helps individuals identify and challenge negative thought patterns and replace them with healthier, more positive thoughts. It is effective for reducing symptoms of anxiety and depression.

This popular form oftherapy is very well known for it's approach to changing the way you think or act to benefit your emotions.
